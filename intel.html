About Intel

Intel is one of the largest multinational technology companies in the United States of America and specializes in computer chips and processors. The company was founded in 1968 as an integrated electronics company headquartered in (Santa Clara, California, America). This company was founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore. Intel is One of the world's largest markets for semiconductor chip makers based on revenue. It is the inventor of the X86 series of processors, which are found in many personal computers. Intel supplies processors and their accessories to computer manufacturers, such as Apple, Hewlett-Packard, Samsung, Dell, and many others. Intel also manufactures chipsets for the motherboard, and Intel produces many small computer parts, including the motherboard and cards. Networks, flash memory, graphics cards, processors, and other devices and parts used in computers. The first microprocessor (commercial microprocessor chip) was made in 1971. Intel is considered one of the companies that contributed to the development of network protocols, the most famous of which is Ethernet.

History

Intel was founded in Mountain View, California, in 1968 by chemist Gordon Moore and physicist Robert Noyce, with help from investor Arthur Rock. Initially, the proposed name for the company was Moore-Noyce, but the inappropriateness of the name prompted the founders to launch the name NM Electronics in 1968, but the name only lasted for a month, and the company was given the name Intel, short for Integrated Electronics. Eight months after the company was founded, Intel launched the 64-bit Schottky 3101 static random-access memory. Only a year later, Intel manufactured the first dynamic random-access memory, the Intel 1103. Intel received its first international order from a mathematical computing company in Japan to manufacture an integrated chip. For Computers, Mathematical Company. While Intel was working on the project, engineer Ted Hoff discovered a way to manufacture the first central processing unit by placing 2,300 transistors in a hand-held chip. The company named it the Intel 4004 and began selling it in 1971.
