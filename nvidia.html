About Nvidia

NVIDIA (NASDAQ: NVDA) is one of the largest companies producing graphics processors, visual display cards, and chipsets for computers and video game systems (Xbox, Sega Saturn, PlayStation 3). It is called a company without a factory and without manufacturing equipment (it designs, but does not manufacture). Its headquarters are located in Santa Clara, California, United States. In addition to manufacturing the GPU, NVIDIA provides parallel processing capabilities to researchers and scientists that allow them to run high-performance applications efficiently. They are deployed at supercomputing sites around the world. More recently, it has moved into the mobile computing market, producing Tegra processors for smartphones and tablets as well as in-car navigation and entertainment systems. In addition to AMD, its competitors include Intel and Qualcomm.

History

NVIDIA was founded on April 5, 1993, by Jin-Sun Huang (CEO as of 2020), a Taiwanese-American, formerly a director of coreware at LSA and a microprocessor designer at AMD, and Chris Malachowski, an... An electrical engineer who worked at Sun Microsystems, Curtis Brehm, previously a senior staff engineer and graphics chip designer at Sun Microsystems. In 1993, the three co-founders believed that the right direction for the next wave of computing was accelerated or graphical computing because it could solve problems that general computing could not. They also noted that video games were simultaneously one of the most computationally challenging and would have an incredibly high sales volume. Both conditions do not occur often. Video games have become the company's flywheel for reaching large markets and funding massive research and development to solve massive computational problems. With only $40,000 in the bank, the company was born. The company subsequently received $20 million in venture capital funding from Sequoia Capital and others. NVIDIA didn't have a name at first and the founders named all their files NV, as in "next release". The need to integrate the company prompted the co-founders to review all words with those two letters, leading them to "invidia", the Latin word for "envy". It was announced by NVIDIA on January 22, 1999
