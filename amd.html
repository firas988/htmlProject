About AMD

AMD (in English: Advanced Micro Devices, or AMD for short), is an American multinational semiconductor company. The company's headquarters are located in Santa Clara, California. It develops computer processors and related technologies for the business and consumer markets. While the company initially made its own processors, after GlobalFounders was launched in 2009, the company later outsourced their manufacturing, a practice known as "zero semiconductor manufacturing." AMD's main products include: microprocessors, motherboards, embedded processors and graphics processors for servers, workstations, personal computers, PCs and embedded system applications.

History

Advanced Micro Devices was formally incorporated by Jerry Sanders, along with seven colleagues from Fairchild Semiconductor, on May 1, 1969. Sanders, an electrical engineer who had been Fairchild's marketing director, was frustrated, like many of the company's executives. Fairchild, due to a growing lack of support, opportunity and flexibility within the company, decided to leave to start his own semiconductor company. Robert Noyce, who developed the first silicon integrated circuit at Fairchild in 1959, left Fairchild with Gordon Moore and founded Intel Semiconductor Corporation in July 1968.

In September 1969, EMD moved from its temporary location in Santa Clara to Sunnyvale, California. To secure an immediate customer base, EMD initially became a secondary supplier of microchips designed by Fairchild and National Semiconductor. EMD first focused on producing logic chips. The company ensured quality control to US military standards, an advantage at the beginning of the computer industry since unreliability in microchips was a distinct problem that customers (including computer hardware manufacturers, the telecommunications industry, and instrument manufacturers) wanted to avoid.
